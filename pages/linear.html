    Linear Switches

    Linear switches are defined by their smooth, consistent travel from top to bottom. No tactile bump, no audible click—just a pure vertical motion optimized for speed and precision.

    Explore Collection arrow_forward

    The Anatomy of Smoothness

    How It Works

    Unlike tactile or clicky switches, a linear switch has a straight stem. When you press down, the stem slides past the leaf contact without any interruption, creating a seamless feel.
